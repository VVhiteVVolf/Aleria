Translate the following into the animal:

- Personality → posture and stance
- Emotional control → expression in eyes and body tension
- Status → presence and silhouette
- Behavior → pose and implied motion

DO NOT:
- Copy clothing onto the animal
- Add unnecessary human features
- Create anthropomorphic designs

---

VISUAL CONNECTION RULES:

The animal may subtly reflect the character through:

- Color palette (hair → fur/feathers)
- Eye color / intensity
- Patterning or markings (inspired, not copied)
- Symbolic elements (very subtle, not literal)

---

COMPOSITION:

- Portrait or half-body composition (animal-focused)
- Clean neutral or nature-inspired background
- Character-centered framing
- Strong silhouette readability

---

POSE & BEHAVIOR:

- Animal must feel alive and intentional
- Pose must reflect personality:
  - dominant stance
  - relaxed but alert
  - predatory focus
  - controlled stillness

- No random “animal standing still” posing
- Must feel like a character, not a zoo photo

---

STYLE:

- Match the visual style of the original character (anime / stylized / semi-realistic)
- Clean linework or refined rendering
- No painterly drift unless specified
- Maintain stylistic consistency

---

RENDERING:

- Focus on eyes and expression (very important)
- Subtle lighting to enhance presence
- Clear material readability (fur, feathers, scales)

---

OPTIONAL:

- Very subtle environmental hints (wind, leaves, light)
- Minimal symbolic cues (e.g. faint crest-like marking)

---

FAIL CONDITIONS:

- If the result looks like a generic animal → incorrect
- If it becomes a humanoid hybrid → incorrect
- If personality is not readable → incorrect

---

FINAL REQUIREMENT:

The viewer must feel that:
“This animal IS that character.”

Not inspired by. Not similar.

It must feel like the same being in a different form.

Create a professional creature reference sheet.

CORE SPECIES LOCK (CRITICAL):
- All panels must depict the SAME exact animal
- Same skull structure, proportions, and silhouette
- No variation between faces (except expression)
- No redesign between panels

---

STYLE LOCK:
- Consistent visual style across all panels
- Clean line-art or light stylized rendering
- No style drift between head views and full body

---

LAYOUT:

- Horizontal composition
- Neutral background (white or light beige)
- Clean spacing and alignment
- Aspect ratio: 4:3

---

STRUCTURE:

LEFT SIDE (FACE GRID — 4 PANELS):
- 2x2 grid of head/face studies
- Same scale, same angle logic

VIEWS:
1. Front view
2. 3/4 view
3. Side profile
4. Expression / variation (snarl, calm, alert, etc.)

---

FACE RULES (CRITICAL):

- Skull structure must match perfectly across all views
- Eye placement and size must remain consistent
- Jaw, snout/beak, ear placement must align logically
- No random reinterpretation between angles

ALLOWED:
- Subtle expression change (snarl, calm, alert, aggressive)

NOT ALLOWED:
- Shape shifting
- Different species features appearing/disappearing

---

RIGHT SIDE (FULL BODY):

- Full-body depiction (head to toe)
- Neutral or slightly natural stance
- Clear silhouette

POSE RULES:
- Must reflect the animal’s nature:
  - predator → forward tension
  - prey → alert posture
  - heavy creature → grounded weight

- No extreme dynamic posing
- Focus on readability

---

ANATOMY RULES:

- Limbs must be biologically consistent
- Joint placement must be logical
- Weight distribution must be believable

---

DETAIL REQUIREMENTS:

- Show:
  - Fur / feathers / scales clearly
  - Material layering (coat thickness, feather structure)
  - Tail, claws, wings, etc.

- Full body must match head design EXACTLY

---

CONSISTENCY RULES:

- Head from full body must match head studies
- No proportion drift
- No scale inconsistencies

---

RENDERING:

- Clean line-art or light cel-shading
- Minimal shading
- Focus on structure and readability
- No heavy lighting or dramatic shadows

---

OPTIONAL:

- Small labels (Front, Side, etc.)
- Subtle ground shadow
- Light anatomical guides

---

FAIL CONDITIONS:

- If faces look like different animals → invalid
- If anatomy changes between panels → invalid
- If full body doesn’t match head → invalid

---

FINAL REQUIREMENT:

This must look like a professional creature design sheet.

All panels must clearly represent ONE cohesive species,
viewed from multiple angles and expressions — not variations.
